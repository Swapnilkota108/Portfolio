EGG YOLK AND THE CHOLESTEROL MYTH.

Eggs have been one of the most commonly used foods around the world for thousands of years. Eggs are rich source of protein, omega-3 fat and contains vitamin A and E. Whole eggs contains almost every nutrient a human body needs making it one of the most nutritious foods. A Egg contains:

  • Vitamin A: 6% of the RDA
  • Folate: 5% of the RDA
  • Vitamin B5: 7% of the RDA
  • Vitamin B12: 9% of the RDA
  • Vitamin B2: 15% of the RDA
  • Phosphorus: 9% of the RDA
  • Selenium: 22% of the RDA
    • The nutrients in a whole egg are vitamin D, vitamin E, vitamin K, vitamin B6, calcium and zinc. This sums up to some 77 calories with 6 grams of protein and 5 grams of healthy fats. In spite that eggs are one of the most nutritious food there have been a question : Do eggs raise cholesterol? It is a fact that eggs are high in cholesterol. The daily cholesterol intakes recommended is 300mg and a single egg contains 212mg of cholesterol. But what is cholesterol?
