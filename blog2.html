EGG YOLK AND THE CHOLESTEROL MYTH.

Eggs have been one of the most commonly used foods around the world for thousands of years. Eggs are rich source of protein, omega-3 fat and contains vitamin A and E. Whole eggs contains almost every nutrient a human body needs making it one of the most nutritious foods. A Egg contains:

  • Vitamin A: 6% of the RDA
  • Folate: 5% of the RDA
  • Vitamin B5: 7% of the RDA
  • Vitamin B12: 9% of the RDA
  • Vitamin B2: 15% of the RDA
  • Phosphorus: 9% of the RDA
  • Selenium: 22% of the RDA
    • Eggs also contain decent amounts of vitamin D, vitamin E, vitamin K, vitamin B6, calcium and zinc. This sums up to some 77 calories with 6 grams of protein and 5 grams of healthy fats. In spite that eggs are one of the most nutritious food there have been a question : Do eggs raise cholesterol? It is a fact that eggs are high in cholesterol. The daily cholesterol intakes recommended is 300mg and a single egg contains 212mg of cholesterol. But what is cholesterol?

    WHAT IS CHOLESTEROL ?

    Blood cholestrol and dietary cholestrol are the two types of cholesterol. High blood cholesterol levels increase heart disease risk, but cholesterol in itself is not necessarily bad. In fact, it’s an essential part of our body’s cells, vitamin D, hormones, and bile acids. Eggs contain dietary cholesterol and this has led to confusion about their impact on heart health. So should you be worried about cholesterol when eating eggs? The latest Heart Foundation recommendations put no limit on how many eggs healthy people can eat each week. The Heart Foundation state that eggs have a minimal effect on blood cholesterol levels and eggs are encouraged as part of a heart healthy eating pattern with a reminder to be mindful of what you choose to eat with them. So even if Eggs are high in cholesterol, eating eggs does not adversely affect cholesterol in the blood for the majority of people.
